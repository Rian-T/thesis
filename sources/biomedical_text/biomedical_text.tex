%Biomedical text is not one thing. The same medical fact, a heart attack, is written down in very different ways depending on who writes it and for whom. A clinician records it in a terse private note, a researcher reports it in a published article, and a patient describes it in plain words on a forum or reads about it in a drug leaflet. These registers differ in their vocabulary, their grammar, and in how freely they can be shared at all. This chapter looks at three of them in turn, moving from the rare and private text of the clinic to the abundant public text of science and the wider web, and asks what each can offer a model meant for clinical use.
