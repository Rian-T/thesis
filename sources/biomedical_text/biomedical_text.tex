\partepigraph{``Language was just difference. A thousand different ways of seeing,
of moving through the world. No; a thousand worlds within one. And
translation---a necessary endeavour, however futile, to move between them.''}{%
R.\,F. Kuang, \textit{Babel, or the Necessity of Violence}}

Medicine does not produce a single kind of text. The same illness is written
about in very different ways, depending on who writes it, who reads it, and what
the writing is meant to do. A hospital note, a research article, and a message on
a patient forum can all describe the same heart attack while sharing almost none
of their words or their aims. The three chapters that follow examine these
registers one by one, returning throughout to a single case, a myocardial
infarction, so the differences stand out. We begin with the clinical note,
because it is the text we ultimately want to model.
