\partepigraph{``Language was just difference. A thousand different ways of seeing,
of moving through the world. No; a thousand worlds within one. And
translation---a necessary endeavour, however futile, to move between them.''}{%
R.\,F. Kuang, \textit{Babel, or the Necessity of Violence}}

Medicine does not produce a single kind of text. The same illness is written
about in very different ways, depending on its writer and its purpose. A hospital
note, a research article, and a message on
a patient forum can all describe the same heart attack while sharing almost none
of their words. The three chapters that follow examine these
registers one by one. They use the same case, a myocardial infarction, to make
the differences easier to see. We begin with the clinical note, because the
extraction tasks in this thesis start there.
