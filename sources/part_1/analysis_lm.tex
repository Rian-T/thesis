Alon Halevy, Peter Norvig, and Fernando Pereira, \textit{The Unreasonable
Effectiveness of Data}}

Clinical text is private, so a public model has to be trained on text gathered
from elsewhere. This part builds that corpus. Collecting biomedical writing is only
the start: a useful corpus also depends on knowing what kind of text it holds and
what is worth keeping. The next three chapters work through this, first assembling a
public French biomedical corpus from open sources, then labelling the kinds of text
it contains down to individual paragraphs, and finally asking which signals of
quality actually improve a model.
