This thesis set out to build language models for French clinical information extraction from public data, without training on real clinical records. The last part brought the earlier work together on one problem. A public biomedical corpus, an encoder trained on it, a training objective with a dose of structured medical knowledge, an open-vocabulary architecture, and data generated rather than collected all met on a single relapsed-lymphoma cohort, and let a small open model that had never seen a real clinical note reach the same level of extraction as a model tuned on a standard benchmark. The model is not yet good enough for the clinic, and the scores stay modest, but the route holds: public text and synthetic data can take a clinical extractor a long way when real data cannot be shared.
